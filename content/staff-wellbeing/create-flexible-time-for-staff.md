## Background from Matt Piggott, Vice Principal and Amy Panayi, Associate Principal

{inset-text}

At Leigh Academy Strood, part of the Leigh Academy Trust, we are deeply committed to building a culture which prioritises the wellbeing of all staff. We recognise that a positive and supportive work environment boosts the morale of individuals as well as the wider school community.

We have implemented strategies that allow staff the flexibility to take some time back and incorporate moments of decompression during the school day.

{/inset-text}

## Wellbeing Wednesdays

All schools in the Leigh Academies Trust finish for students at 2:00pm every Wednesday to build staff CPD time into the academy day and directed time calendar.

Schools held ‘Wellbeing Wednesdays’ on the last Wednesday of each half term to support staff wellbeing. Some schools had organised wellbeing activities for staff after the students have left, such as yoga or mindfulness sessions.

Feedback from our staff surveys showed that staff preferred to use this time in a way that best suits their own personal needs. Staff at Leigh Academy Strood can now finish early on ‘Wellbeing Wednesdays’ instead and choose how they use the time. Making this change reinforced our commitment to wellbeing in a more balanced and flexible way.

### No break duties

No teachers at Leigh Academy Strood have break duties. Instead, we have significantly invested in our pastoral team who, alongside the senior leadership team, take responsibility for these duties.

Teachers can use their breaks and lunchtimes to decompress, attend to personal needs, or collaborate with colleagues, without the added pressure of supervision roles.

Pastoral staff who carry out lunchtime duties can choose either a free lunch or a lunch break at a different time during their working day.

### Golden ticket

Each staff member receives 3 golden tickets per year, each ticket entitling them to 2 hours of paid time off. These can be used for personal commitments, such as attending their child’s sports day or managing other important life events. All staff can benefit from this approach.

Staff are asked to submit their golden ticket(s) alongside their leave application at least 48 hours in advance to help with cover planning. The scheme is designed to be inclusive, allowing staff to benefit from time off regardless of the reason. Additionally, staff have the option of combining all three Golden Tickets to take a full day off, offering even greater flexibility.

We have sufficient teaching staff that we can allocate each teacher slightly above the minimum 10% requirement for planning, preparation, and assessment time. We have also been able to employ a second cover supervisor. We can therefore guarantee that, even if they cover lessons occasionally, teachers still get at least 10% of their timetable as non-contact time. We can cover short staff absences without using supply teachers, which helps to reduce costs.
